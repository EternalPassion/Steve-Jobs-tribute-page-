Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs adressing an event of his company 'Apple'

Steve Jobs, co-founder of Apple Inc., was a visionary entrepreneur whose innovations transformed the tech industry. Born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, he was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs. Jobs dropped out of Reed College but continued auditing classes, fostering his interest in design and technology. In 1976, he co-founded Apple in his garage with Steve Wozniak, creating the Apple I. Jobs' emphasis on sleek design and user experience led to revolutionary products like the iPhone, iPad, and MacBook. Despite a tumultuous career, including a period away from Apple, his return in 1997 marked the company's resurgence, cementing his legacy as a pioneer in personal computing and mobile technology.
